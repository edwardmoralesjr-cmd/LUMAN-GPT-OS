# 4. Chapter Continuity Summaries

## Chapter 1: Stone and Silence

Auron wakes in contemporary Cairnwake before full day and observes the city in its most honest state, where modern infrastructure and old stone coexist uneasily. In the Hearthring, he finds a partially hidden carved mark in the retaining wall beneath the Stone Registry after fresh covering material flakes away. When he brushes it clear, he experiences impossible recognition tied to a stair in darkness, niche lamps, and an ancient place he should not know. Liora steadies him. Solren arrives. When the Registry bell sounds, all three hear a second, older tone beneath it. Something beneath the city answers, and no one else seems to react.

## Chapter 2: What Lingers Unnamed

By midmorning, the city resumes ordinary behavior, which unsettles Auron further. He tries to move through routine and notices a remembrance lamp lit in fear rather than custom. Liora meets him at an overlook and helps him name that the wall did not merely frighten him, it recognized him. She admits she also heard the second tone. Solren discovers additional buried seams near the Old Quarter and toward the Lantern Stair. The trio realizes the Registry is covering signs as fast as they appear through ordinary maintenance procedures. On Lantern Stair, they sense that even the sound of the city behaves differently there. The chapter closes with the realization that whatever answered once beneath the visible city is still listening.

## Chapter 3: The Old Quarter Rumor

The mystery moves from private anomaly into socially embedded unease. The trio investigates the Old Stone Quarter and meets Maela Velt, an elder lamp repairer and keeper of local rumor memory. Maela confirms that the second tone beneath the Registry bell predates the trio and is part of inherited Old Quarter warning tradition. She points them toward Stonemasons Court and the old wash-house arch, where a repair crew has covered something too quickly and too carefully. The trio discovers a partially revealed mark beneath fresh dust. Auron feels a pulse from the exposed lines without touching them. Registry-linked personnel approach almost immediately, forcing the trio to withdraw.

## Chapter 4: The Mark Beneath Dust

The Stonemasons Court mark becomes partially preserved by memory and sketch. A partial four-stroke version now exists in Solren’s notebook. The act of completing or preserving the mark carries immediate cost for Auron, triggering a nosebleed, brief dissociation, and loss of present-time continuity. Auron receives a stronger fragmentary threshold impression tied to stone steps, niche lamps, one unlit lamp below a turn, the second tone, and a nonverbal sense of direct address. Registry-linked cover procedure now includes rapid public-facing closure notices in municipal systems. Civic parcel and infrastructure layers flatten or omit Stonemasons Court and the wash-house arch area rather than representing the visible location clearly.
