# Infinite Bloom Music — Sonic Canon

## Status

Current primary Working Canon as of 2026-07-21.

This file governs the main Infinite Bloom music project unless Edward explicitly revises it.

## Core Identity

Original modern cinematic metalcore / alternative metal with a mythic spiritual identity rooted in the Infinite Bloom and Roseborn cosmology.

The sound must be beautiful but brutal, wounded yet transcendent, cinematic, memorable, and fully original.

## Sonic Balance

Build extreme contrast between fragile atmosphere and explosive heaviness.

The music should combine:

- rich oppressive distortion
- low-tuned technical and articulated guitars
- punchy modern drums
- deep physically dominant bass
- dark ambient textures
- emotional melodic leads
- silence and negative space
- controlled dissonance
- evolving instrumental and vocal breakdowns

## Vocals

Primary vocal range:

- soulful polished vulnerable cleans
- intimate close-mic confession
- emotionally charged rising melody
- raw controlled harsh vocals
- restrained volatile shouts
- piercing or sustained screams when narratively earned

The vocalist should feel like a human being passing through revelation, collapse, responsibility, memory, and rebirth.

Do not default the entire project to scream-only vocals unless Edward explicitly chooses that approach for a song.

## Guitars

Use:

- low tuning
- rich layered saturation
- technical melodic development
- dissonant chords
- scraping bends
- harmonic shrieks
- feedback swells
- expressive lead guitar
- rhythmic impacts shaped by silence
- instrumental breakdowns that evolve rather than repeat

Technical playing must serve emotion and mythic narrative.

Avoid sterile shredding and repetitive chug cycles.

## Rhythm and Bass

Drums should remain punchy, physical, modern, and alive.

Use:

- anxious syncopation
- stop-start half-time
- double-kick displacement
- dynamic transitions
- evolving breakdowns
- abrupt silence
- climactic rhythmic escalation

Bass should be deep, overdriven, physically dominant, and integrated into the wall of sound rather than hidden beneath the guitars.

## Atmosphere

Use dark ambient textures, room tone, decaying guitar trails, sacred-space resonance, subtle environmental sound, and cinematic transitions.

Atmosphere should suggest ancient architecture, buried memory, sacred fire, fracture, descent, and awakening without relying on generic fantasy scoring.

## Lyrical Center

Primary themes:

- the Infinite Bloom
- the Fracture
- the Spiral
- the Rose
- sacred fire
- descent
- remembrance
- suffering as refinement
- responsibility
- inner collapse
- rebirth
- the twelve dragons and their laws
- unity without erasing difference

Lyrics should feel poetic, mythic, spiritual, dark, embodied, and emotionally human.

Do not write every song as exposition. Each song should embody a law or transformation through lived emotional stakes.

## Structural Law

There is no fixed universal structure.

Every song must have its own:

- intro identity
- pacing
- vocal entrance
- verse or movement logic
- chorus timing
- breakdown placement
- instrumental development
- climax
- ending

Do not repeat a standard intro -> verse -> pre-chorus -> chorus -> verse -> breakdown -> final chorus template across the project.

A song may use a familiar form only when its emotional function requires it and its production identity remains distinct.

## Technical Limits

- Suno style prompt: 1,000 characters or less
- Lyrics: below 5,000 characters unless Edward explicitly changes the limit
- Unique composition architecture required for every song

## Exclusions

Avoid:

- choir
- operatic vocals
- EDM drops
- generic trap beats
- glitch effects as a default identity
- repetitive chugging
- sterile shredding
- flat dynamics
- formulaic arrangements
- generic metalcore cliches
- predictable repetition

## Superseded Direction

The earlier dark cinematic trap-metal / classical-composition direction is preserved as an archived experiment.

It may inform a future song or alternate movement, but it does not govern the current primary Infinite Bloom sound.

## First Song Reference

`The Fire That Named Me`

The song should function as an Elarion / First Flame opening statement in which flame is not merely power; it is responsibility awakened through suffering.
