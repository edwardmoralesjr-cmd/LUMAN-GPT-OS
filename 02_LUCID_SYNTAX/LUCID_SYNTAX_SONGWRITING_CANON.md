# Lucid Syntax Songwriting Canon

## Status

Locked reusable songwriting and sonic standard as of 2026-07-21.

This file governs future Lucid Syntax songwriting unless Edward explicitly revises the style for a specific song or album.

## Core Sonic Identity

Modern cinematic metalcore / alternative metal fused with trap-glitch architecture and a hyper-distinct sonic identity.

The music should feel like intimate human breakdown inside mechanical ruin.

## Vocal Architecture

Lower male vocals morph continuously through:

- intimate close-mic confession
- precise rapid-fire rap fragments
- restrained but volatile shouts
- haunting fragile cleans near collapse
- raw controlled screams when the emotional architecture requires them

Vocal performances should feel story-driven, psychologically unstable, spiritual, raw, controlled, and painfully human.

Avoid:

- choir
- operatic vocals
- generic screams
- flat delivery
- emotionless vocal acrobatics

## Guitar Architecture

Use:

- low-tuned dissonant guitars
- technical articulated phrasing
- layered distortion and saturation
- scraping bends
- harmonic shrieks
- feedback swells
- pick noise
- negative space
- rhythmic violence
- tension that resolves only when emotionally earned

Avoid:

- generic repetitive chugging
- sterile shredding
- technique without emotional function
- predictable riff cycles

## Rhythm Architecture

Drums should remain sharp, modern, kinetic, and dynamically alive.

Use:

- fractured stop-start half-time
- anxious syncopation
- double-kick displacement
- sudden pressure changes
- evolving breakdowns
- silence as a rhythmic weapon
- breakdowns that change rather than loop

Sub bass should be deep, distorted, oppressive, and physically present.

## Atmosphere

Build the environment from:

- ambient synth haze
- reversed textures
- glitch artifacts
- static
- digital decay
- amplifier hum
- decaying guitar trails
- collapsing layers
- room noise
- abrupt silence cuts

## Structural Law

Every song must have its own identity.

Do not repeat:

- intro type
- sonic opening setup
- vocal entrance
- verse-to-chorus timing
- chorus placement
- breakdown position
- pacing pattern
- ending structure
- atmospheric progression

Structures should be unpredictable, asymmetrical, cinematic, and emotionally necessary rather than random.

No fixed universal arrangement is permitted.

## Lyric Architecture

Write lyrics with the sonic style already embedded in:

- line length
- breath placement
- rhythmic density
- silence
- rapid-fire passages
- clean-vocal openings
- scream transitions
- breakdown language
- emotional escalation

Lyrics should remain unique from song to song.

Avoid repeated dependence on:

- `teeth`
- clocks or time imagery
- bills or the same work-life objects
- recycled everyday metaphors
- genre-cliche declarations

## Technical Limits

- Suno style prompts: 1,000 characters or less
- Lyrics: below 5,000 characters unless Edward explicitly changes the limit
- Song duration target: under four minutes unless Edward asks otherwise
- Unique intro required for every song

## Arrangement-Only Edits

When Edward says to keep every lyric line unchanged:

- preserve every lyric line exactly
- change only performance, production, and arrangement cues
- do not rewrite wording, punctuation, or sequence unless he explicitly permits it

## Project Boundary

This canon belongs to Lucid Syntax.

Do not automatically apply it to:

- Infinite Bloom music
- Damaged Grammar
- unrelated metal experiments
- one-off scream-only or Viking-vocal experiments

Those projects require their own governing sonic files.

## Current Album Priority

`Visionary` remains the active shipping project.

`The Human Syntax` is confirmed as Lucid Syntax Album 5, but future-album development must not displace Visionary release execution.
