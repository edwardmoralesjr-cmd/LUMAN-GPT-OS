## Master Structure

The Roseborn Universe is organized as a 21-book master series across five movements.

```text
Movement I   Fracture War Duology        2 books
Movement II  Roseborn Saga Decalogy     10 books
Movement III Silent Cycle Trilogy        3 books
Movement IV  Grand Generals Pentalogy    5 books
Movement V   The Third Emergence         1 book
                                           ------
                                           21 books
```

Chronological order and reader-entry order are not the same. Grand Generals remains the grounded reader entry point even though the deeper history begins with the Fracture War.

# Movement I: Fracture War Duology

## 1. The Veiled Dissonance

Final age before collapse. Doctrinal strain, Choir influence, sacred political division, and Myrrak's deepening dissent weaken the Bloom Concord.

## 2. The Codex Fracture

Myrrak's betrayal, the Fracture War, collapse of sacred order, and the wounding of memory and time.

# Movement II: Roseborn Saga Decalogy

## 3. The Wounded Petal

A central lineage begins awakening through broken symbol, inherited wound, and incomplete sacred memory.

## 4. The Thorn of Memory

Lineage, memory, and the cost of inheritance become active forces in the struggle.

## 5. The Mirror of Ash

The protagonists confront rupture, corrupted legacy, and truths that survived the Fracture in distorted form.

## 6. The Spiral Crown

Time, recurrence, destiny, and Aurelith's spiral logic move to the center of the saga.

## 7. The Rootfire Covenant

Memory, vow, sacred alliance, and living lineage braid together under pressure.

## 8. The House of Broken Crests

Dragon Houses and sacred lineages return in fractured, competing, and partially corrupted forms.

## 9. The Bloom Without Mercy

Awakening intensifies and proves dangerous as well as holy. Growth without integration becomes its own threat.

## 10. The Sovereign Ruin

Sovereignty is confronted through sacrifice, responsibility, and the collapse of false power.

## 11. The Living Codex

A Living Codex response changes the structure of the struggle and redefines what sacred texts can become.

## 12. The Roseborn Flame

Roseborn identity becomes active, embodied, and publicly consequential. The Decalogy reaches its high-myth culmination.

# Movement III: Silent Cycle Trilogy

## 13. The Quiet Seals

Incomplete awakening enters a secretive and unstable aftermath. New truth is contained because society cannot yet integrate it safely.

## 14. The Names That Remain

Memory, identity, lineage, and the right to preserve a name become the battlefield.

## 15. The Silence Before Return

Concealment becomes unsustainable. Old powers reposition and the conditions for grounded reawakening emerge.

# Movement IV: Grand Generals Pentalogy

## 16. Grand Generals I: The Waking Stone

Auron's unrest reveals Cairnwake's buried sacred architecture. Haunted normality becomes undeniable proof.

## 17. Grand Generals II: The Broken Registry

Cairnwake's managed memory begins to fail. Civic reality is exposed as a structure built on controlled distortion.

## 18. Grand Generals III: The Gray Verge

The mystery expands beyond the protected center into the outer ruin belt, remnant routes, and consequences the Registry could not fully contain.

## 19. Grand Generals IV: The Sovereign Threshold

Auron, Liora, and Solren confront the deeper meaning of sovereignty, recognition, burden, and irreversible passage.

## 20. Grand Generals V: The Grand Generals

The grounded branch reaches its full identity. The trio and the wider movement become capable of entering the final convergence as active agents rather than witnesses.

# Movement V: The Third Emergence

## 21. The Third Emergence

The final convergence of the Codices, dragons, Roseborn, Grand Generals, Ascendants, Auracites, Choir of the Unshaped, Mortal Primacy, and the Living Flame.

The finale must emerge through accumulated character choice and cost, not cosmological spectacle alone.

# Reserved Expansion Titles

```text
The Academy of Petals
The Last Bloom Before Silence
```

These titles are preserved as Reserved Expansion Material. They are not deleted and may later become:

- Interlude novels
- Companion novels
- Academy branch books
- Novellas
- Era or arc labels
- Replacement titles if the Decalogy is intentionally revised

They are not part of the active numbered 21-book architecture unless Edward explicitly reactivates them.
