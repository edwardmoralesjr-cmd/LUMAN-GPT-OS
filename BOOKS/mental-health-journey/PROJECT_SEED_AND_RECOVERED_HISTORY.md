# Mental Health Journey Book — Project Seed and Recovered History

## State

Incubating personal nonfiction project. Final title unresolved.

## Recovery and Privacy Note

This public repository file preserves the book's themes and architecture without publishing detailed medical records, medication data, private family material, or clinical claims.

## Core Purpose

Tell the honest story of Edward's mental-health journey and connect it to creativity, spirituality, work, family, treatment, suffering, meaning, and the creation of the Codices.

## Recovered Themes

- Depression and periods of feeling deeply down
- Crying spells and emotional overwhelm
- Mania and unusually intense creative production
- Medication and the uncertainty of finding the right treatment
- The realization that many Codices were written within a short period following mania
- Spiritual awakening and meaning-making
- The danger of romanticizing suffering
- The reality that art can emerge from pain without making pain desirable
- Marriage, fatherhood, responsibility, and the wish for stability
- Choosing a lower-pressure life or work arrangement for mental health
- Transforming suffering into poetry, music, doctrine, and practical systems

## Core Ethical Position

The book should neither reduce Edward's creative work to illness nor use spirituality to deny clinical reality.

It should hold both truths:

- Mental-health conditions require grounded care and responsibility.
- Lived suffering can still be transformed into meaning, art, compassion, and self-knowledge.
