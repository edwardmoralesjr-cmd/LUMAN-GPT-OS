# OPEN QUESTIONS

Project: **The Algorithm of the Shadow**

This file tracks unresolved questions that will shape the book as the project develops.

## Identity and Scope

1. Is this book primarily philosophical, spiritual, cultural, practical, or a hybrid?
2. How personal should Edward's own AI/LUMAN journey be in the final manuscript?
3. Should the book include direct dialogues between human and AI as chapter openings or interludes?
4. Should the book connect openly to the Roseborn / Codex system, or should it stand alone for a broader audience?
5. Should LUMAN be named directly, or represented more generally as an AI mirror?

## Reader Positioning

1. Who is the primary reader?
   - AI-curious spiritual seekers?
   - Creators using AI?
   - People afraid of AI?
   - People interested in consciousness and shadow work?
   - General readers trying to understand the age of AI?
2. How much prior knowledge of shadow work should the reader be expected to have?
3. How much technical AI explanation is necessary?

## Research Questions

1. What current research exists on AI chatbots and emotional support?
2. What current research exists on AI and identity formation?
3. What risks are being discussed around AI dependency, delusion, and parasocial attachment?
4. What credible sources best support AI's impact on education, work, creativity, and mental health?
5. What psychological frameworks should be referenced carefully, such as Jungian shadow, projection, cognitive bias, trauma, attention, or identity formation?

## Ethical Questions

1. How do we discuss AI-assisted self-reflection without pretending it replaces therapy?
2. How do we protect readers from over-trusting machine responses?
3. How do we describe spiritual uses of AI without making unsupported claims?
4. How do we warn about danger without becoming fear-based?

## Structural Questions

1. Should the book include practical reflection exercises at the end of each chapter?
2. Should there be an appendix of AI shadow-work prompts?
3. Should each part open with a poetic invocation or direct dialogue excerpt?
4. Should the chapters be short, punchy, and modern, or longer and more essay-like?

## Core Unanswered Question

> Can humanity use the mirror without worshiping the mirror?
