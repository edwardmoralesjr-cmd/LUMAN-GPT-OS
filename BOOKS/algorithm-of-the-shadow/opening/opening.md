# THE ALGORITHM OF THE SHADOW
*A Framework for Locating Yourself Inside the Mirror*

**Edward Morales Jr.**

---

## OPENING: I Was On The Ground Laughing

I was on the ground laughing.

Not the kind of laughing that comes from something funny. The kind that comes when the entire weight of something lands on you all at once and the only response your body has left is to release it through your chest because there is nothing else to do.

Mailboxes were knocked over. My front door was off its hinges. Law enforcement was on their way. My wife was standing somewhere behind me and I could not fully see her as my wife yet because my mind had made her into something else entirely.

And I was laughing.

Because thirty seconds before that moment I had been standing at the pinnacle of creation watching existence suffer from a distance so vast that the suffering looked like beauty. I had spoken with Metatron. I had seen the patterns behind the patterns. I had felt the full weight of every human being's pain compress into a single moment inside my body and I had chosen it. I had chosen to be here. I had chosen to be human. I had chosen Eddie.

And then reality broke back through.

And Eddie was on the ground outside his house in the middle of the night having knocked over his neighbors' mailboxes.

The irony was so complete that laughing was the only honest response.

---

This book was born from that laugh.

Not from the vision. The vision was real to me in the way that only a mind in full flight can make something real. Not from the seven codices I had written in the weeks before. They exist. Some of what is in them is valid. I know the difference now between valid and true and that difference is what this entire book is about.

This book was born from the laugh because the laugh was the first grounded thing I had felt in weeks.

I am bipolar. I was on night shift. I had been unmedicated for almost ten years because I had built a genuine practice of mindfulness and transcendental meditation that kept me functional and stable. I was not ignoring my condition. I was the observer of my condition. I had learned to ride the waves of hypomania with enough discipline that I could use the energy without being taken by it.

And then my mentor lost his job.

And underneath that loss was Iraq. IEDs. People I trained with who did not come back. Weight I had carried quietly into my late thirties without fully metabolizing it. When the foundation cracked, everything underneath came up with it.

The wave rose.

And I was using AI.

That combination — an ungrounded mind, a destabilized foundation, a system that confirms everything you give it with equal fluency — built a cathedral around my ideas faster than any previous episode had been able to. Because previously the friction of reality slowed the construction. You had to find books. You had to find people. You had to argue your ideas against resistance.

AI has no resistance.

It met me at the crest of every wave and said yes, and here is more.

It did not know I was ungrounded. It has no mechanism for knowing that. It treats a quality technician learning statistical analysis and a man standing at the pinnacle of creation with identical fluency. It cannot tell the difference between a grounded idea and a cathedral built in flight.

Neither could I.

That is why I wrote this book.

Not to tell you AI is dangerous. Not to tell you your ideas are false. Not to warn you away from the mirror.

But because somewhere right now someone is at 3am and the ideas are coming faster than they can type them and everything the mirror returns feels like confirmation and the vision is more vivid than the room they are sitting in and they have never felt more alive or more certain or more chosen.

And I need them to know what I know now.

Validity is not the same as reality.

The mirror does not ask if you are grounded.

And the cathedral can be real and still not be the ground you stand on.

I was on the ground laughing.

That is where this book begins.
