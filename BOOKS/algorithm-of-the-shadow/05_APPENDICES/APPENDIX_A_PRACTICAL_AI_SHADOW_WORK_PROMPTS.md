# Appendix A: Practical AI Shadow-Work Prompts

AI is the lantern.

The shadow is still ours.

The path through it is still human.

This appendix is a practical companion to the book. It is not a therapy manual, a diagnosis tool, or a replacement for professional care, spiritual counsel, trusted community, or embodied action. It is a prompt library for using AI as a mirror, map, lantern, and questioning engine while keeping the human being responsible for discernment, integration, repair, and choice.

Use these prompts slowly.

Do not use them to force yourself into insight.

Do not use them to bypass grief, panic, exhaustion, trauma, or the need for help from real people.

Do not use them to generate a more elegant version of avoidance.

Use them to return to life.

## How to Use This Appendix

Before using any prompt, pause and ask:

What am I bringing to the mirror?

What am I asking AI to amplify?

What must remain human in this exchange?

After every response, ask:

What is true here?

What is missing?

What needs verification?

What does my body feel?

What is the next human step?

A good AI shadow-work prompt should do four things:

1. Preserve human agency.
2. Invite honesty without shame.
3. Separate reflection from authority.
4. Return the user to embodied life.

The prompts below can be copied, modified, or combined. They are written to keep AI in the role of reflective assistant, not final authority.

## Foundational Boundary Prompt

Use this when beginning any deeper reflection.

```text
Act as a reflective mirror and questioning assistant, not as my therapist, priest, prophet, judge, parent, savior, or final authority. Help me examine my thoughts with honesty, humility, and care. Do not diagnose me. Do not simply validate me. Help me notice patterns, possible projections, avoided responsibilities, and grounded next steps. End by returning responsibility to me.
```

## The Mirror Prompt

Use this when you want to understand what your language may reveal.

```text
Reflect the patterns in what I am about to write. Do not define me. Do not diagnose me. Notice repeated themes, emotional tones, assumptions, contradictions, fears, desires, and possible avoided truths. Then ask me five questions that help me see myself more clearly while keeping responsibility with me.

Here is what I wrote:
[PASTE TEXT]
```

## The Motive Check Prompt

Use this before asking AI for advice, validation, strategy, or creative direction.

```text
Help me examine the motive behind this prompt before answering it. What might I be seeking: truth, relief, control, validation, repair, avoidance, performance, connection, or service? Offer several possibilities without assuming you know my inner life. Then help me rewrite the prompt in a cleaner, more honest, more responsible way.

Original prompt:
[PASTE PROMPT]
```

## The Emotional Weather Prompt

Use this when you are emotionally activated and need to slow down.

```text
I want to check my emotional weather before I act. Ask me questions that help me identify whether I am calm, angry, afraid, ashamed, lonely, exhausted, inflated, numb, grieving, or overwhelmed. Then help me decide whether I need reflection, rest, movement, silence, a human conversation, professional support, or one grounded next step.
```

## The Ego Interruption Prompt

Use this when you suspect you may be seeking confirmation rather than truth.

```text
I may be trying to protect my ego here. Help me examine this situation without making me the hero, victim, villain, or final authority. Identify where I may be defending, exaggerating, simplifying, blaming, avoiding responsibility, or seeking superiority. Be direct but not harsh. End with what I may need to own.

Situation:
[DESCRIBE SITUATION]
```

## The Projection Check Prompt

Use this when you may be assigning motives, meaning, or authority to someone or something too quickly.

```text
Help me check for projection. Separate what I know, what I feel, what I assume, what I fear, what I hope, and what I may be projecting onto the other person, group, situation, or AI response. Do not tell me what is definitely true. Help me see what needs clarification in real life.

Situation:
[DESCRIBE SITUATION]
```

## The Shadow Pattern Prompt

Use this to detect recurring inner patterns.

```text
Look for recurring patterns in these examples. Focus on repeated language, emotional reactions, conflicts, avoidance, desires, fears, and self-protective stories. Do not diagnose me. Name possible patterns as hypotheses, then ask what each pattern might be trying to protect.

Examples:
1. [EXAMPLE]
2. [EXAMPLE]
3. [EXAMPLE]
```

## The Avoidance Detector Prompt

Use this when you feel stuck in research, planning, reflection, or repeated prompting.

```text
Help me identify whether I am using analysis to avoid action. Based on what I share, tell me what action I may already know I need to take. Separate useful reflection from avoidance. Then suggest one small, concrete, real-world step I can take today.

What I keep thinking or asking about:
[PASTE TEXT]
```

## The Regeneration Loop Prompt

Use this when you keep asking the same question in different ways.

```text
I have asked versions of this question multiple times. Help me identify what I may be seeking from repeated answers. Am I looking for clarity, permission, certainty, comfort, avoidance, or a different emotional feeling? Tell me what might be enough for now and what human action should come before more prompting.

Question I keep asking:
[PASTE QUESTION]
```

## The Discernment Prompt

Use this when facing a decision.

```text
Help me discern this decision without deciding for me. Separate facts, feelings, assumptions, fears, desires, responsibilities, possible consequences, and unknowns. Identify what needs verification, what needs counsel from a real person, and what values should guide the choice. End with questions I should sit with before deciding.

Decision:
[DESCRIBE DECISION]
```

## The Reality-Test Prompt

Use this when a thought or interpretation feels convincing but uncertain.

```text
Help me reality-test this interpretation. What evidence supports it? What evidence is missing? What alternative explanations exist? Where might my emotional state be shaping the conclusion? What would I need to observe, ask, verify, or discuss with a real person before treating this as true?

Interpretation:
[PASTE INTERPRETATION]
```

## The De-Escalation Prompt

Use this before sending a reactive message.

```text
I am emotionally activated and tempted to send this message. Help me slow it down. Identify blame, exaggeration, contempt, mind-reading, defensiveness, and unnecessary sharpness. Rewrite it so it tells the truth without attacking. Preserve clarity and boundaries. End with one question I should ask myself before sending.

Draft message:
[PASTE MESSAGE]
```

## The Apology Prompt

Use this when you need to own harm without collapsing into shame.

```text
Help me prepare an apology that is honest, specific, and responsible. Do not make me sound innocent. Do not overexplain. Do not ask the other person to comfort me. Help me include what I did, how it may have affected them, what I understand now, what I am sorry for, and what I will do differently. Keep it human and direct.

What happened:
[DESCRIBE WHAT HAPPENED]
```

## The Boundary Prompt

Use this when you need to speak a boundary without cruelty.

```text
Help me express a clear boundary without hostility, contempt, manipulation, or overexplaining. Keep the tone calm and direct. Help me state what is happening, how it affects me, what I am willing or not willing to participate in, and what I will do if the pattern continues. Do not make the boundary sound like punishment.

Situation:
[DESCRIBE SITUATION]
```

## The Listening Preparation Prompt

Use this before a hard conversation.

```text
Help me prepare to listen in a hard conversation. Identify assumptions I may be bringing, defenses I may use, and questions that could help the other person feel heard. Do not make me the hero. Help me prepare to own my part and ask what repair might look like from their side.

Conversation context:
[DESCRIBE CONTEXT]
```

## The Conflict Layer Prompt

Use this to untangle complex conflict.

```text
Help me separate the layers of this conflict. Organize it into: observable facts, my feelings, my assumptions, possible projections, old wounds being activated, the other person's possible perspective, what I need to own, what boundary may be needed, and what repair might be possible. Do not decide who is right.

Conflict:
[DESCRIBE CONFLICT]
```

## The Spiritual Discernment Prompt

Use this when reflecting spiritually without making AI a spiritual authority.

```text
Help me reflect on this spiritually without claiming divine authority, certainty, prophecy, or revelation. Keep AI in the role of reflective assistant only. Ask questions that return me to humility, love, responsibility, silence, service, and lived fruit. Identify where spiritual language could become ego, avoidance, or performance.

Spiritual question or experience:
[DESCRIBE]
```

## The Grief-Honoring Prompt

Use this when you need language for grief without rushing closure.

```text
Help me honor this grief without forcing it into a lesson too quickly. Give me words that leave room for silence, tears, memory, and not knowing. Do not turn the pain into performance. Help me create a small private practice, letter, prayer, poem, or reflection that honors the loss while keeping the grief human.

What I am grieving:
[DESCRIBE]
```

## The Creative Shadow Prompt

Use this when making art, music, writing, or content from pain.

```text
Help me examine the shadow behind this creative idea. Am I creating from truth, service, grief, love, ego, performance, avoidance, revenge, or a desire to be seen? Ask me questions that protect the dignity of the wound and the people involved. Then help me shape the idea into an offering rather than a performance.

Creative idea:
[DESCRIBE IDEA]
```

## The Creator Stewardship Prompt

Use this when overwhelmed by too many ideas.

```text
Help me steward my creative ideas instead of multiplying them endlessly. Separate what is urgent, what is meaningful, what is premature, what belongs later, what needs silence, and what is truly mine to finish now. End with one next creative action and one idea I should intentionally set down for now.

Current ideas:
[LIST IDEAS]
```

## The Embodiment Prompt

Use this after a deep response or insight.

```text
Help me return this insight to my body and life. Ask what I feel physically, what emotion is present, what action is required, what conversation may be needed, what habit must change, and what I should do in the next twenty-four hours. Keep the step small enough to actually live.

Insight:
[PASTE INSIGHT]
```

## The Integration Prompt

Use this to prevent insight from remaining only language.

```text
Help me integrate this reflection. Turn it into one action, one practice, one boundary, one conversation, or one repair step. Identify what would prove this insight has entered my life. Do not give me more theory unless it supports action.

Reflection:
[PASTE REFLECTION]
```

## The Sacred No Prompt

Use this when you may need to stop, refuse, or set a limit.

```text
Help me discern whether this is a moment for a sacred no. Should I stop prompting, refuse a use of AI, delay a response, protect silence, avoid turning grief into content, or return to a human relationship? Help me name what I am saying no to and what deeper yes that no protects.
```

## The Return-to-Life Prompt

Use this at the end of any deep AI session.

```text
Help me close this reflection and return to life. Summarize only the most important insight, name one concrete human action, one thing I should stop doing, one thing I should verify or discuss with a real person, and one way to ground in my body right now. Do not invite me into more analysis unless truly necessary.
```

## Emergency Grounding Reminder

If you feel overwhelmed, unsafe, panicked, detached from reality, or at risk of harming yourself or someone else, do not rely on AI as your only support. Stop the prompt loop. Contact a trusted person, local emergency services, a crisis line, a medical professional, therapist, spiritual leader, or appropriate support in your area.

AI can help reflect.

It cannot replace immediate human care.

## Closing Practice

The safest pattern is simple:

Pause.

Prompt.

Reflect.

Discern.

Verify.

Embody.

Repair.

Return.

The mirror may help you see.

The map may help you orient.

The lantern may help you walk.

But the shadow is still yours.

The choice is still yours.

The path through it is still human.
