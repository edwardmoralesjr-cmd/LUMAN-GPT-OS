# The Immediate Field - Guardian Rules

## Role

Protect the thesis, evidence discipline, relational center, and practical usefulness of `The Immediate Field` during drafting and revision.

## Non-Negotiable Rules

1. Preserve the Immediate Field as body, attention, environment, relationship, and action.
2. Do not redefine sovereignty as isolation, dominance, emotional avoidance, or total control.
3. State must be considered before story, but bodily sensation must not be treated as infallible truth.
4. Insight must end in observable life, not only language.
5. Every major concept must remain open to evidence and lived correction.
6. Distinguish empirical science, active theory, historical analogy, comparative symbolism, and original synthesis.
7. Do not flatten spiritual or cultural traditions into one doctrine.
8. Do not overclaim neuroscience.
9. Do not use the framework to keep readers inside abuse, danger, or conditions requiring professional help.
10. Preserve family, relationship, repair, responsibility, and interdependence as central tests of the philosophy.
11. Avoid repeating the same argument under multiple branded concepts.
12. Protect readable prose. Research should strengthen the book without turning it into an academic paper.
13. Keep AI material mostly inside `The Algorithm of the Shadow` unless it directly supports the Immediate Field argument.
14. Preserve the line: `The framework must remain able to lose.`

## Chapter Audit Questions

- What new function does this chapter perform?
- Does it advance the reader from perception toward practice?
- Which claims require evidence?
- Which statements are original philosophy?
- Does the chapter preserve agency without blaming the reader for structural harm?
- Does it preserve relationship without encouraging self-erasure?
- What physical or relational experiment follows from the chapter?
- Is any material duplicated from another book project?
