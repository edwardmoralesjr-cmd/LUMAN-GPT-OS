# Run Your Own Code - Revised Research-Informed Framework

## Status

Recovered source snapshot of the revised research-informed paper that directly preceded the decision to develop *The Immediate Field* as a book. This file is a structured provenance record, not the governing manuscript.

## Original Thesis

Human beings inherit biological survival systems, narrative identities, cultural scripts, institutional meanings, and relational patterns before they develop the capacity to examine them. Conscious living requires an internal process for regulating state, separating observation from interpretation, testing narratives, selecting action, and revising through consequence.

## Epistemic Discipline

The revised framework established four knowledge categories:

1. Empirical evidence: claims supported by research and stated within the limits of that research.
2. Historical interpretation: qualified examples and analogies rather than universal laws.
3. Comparative symbol: traditions used carefully without claiming doctrinal equivalence.
4. Original synthesis and practice: the author's conceptual language and testable personal protocols.

The full synthesis was explicitly described as research-informed rather than an empirically validated complete theory.

## Major Concepts

### Survival Hardware

- Threat detection
- Belonging
- Status
- Pleasure and pain regulation
- Energy conservation
- Pattern recognition
- Predictive preparation

### Narrative Software

- Family roles
- Cultural expectations
- Economic assumptions
- Religious and spiritual meaning
- Institutional legitimacy
- Memory and identity
- Media and algorithmic influence

### Narrative Capture Chain

```text
Exposure -> Activation -> Interpretation -> Identity Claim -> Behavioral Command -> Reinforcement
```

### Core Directive

> I am already worthy, and from that worthiness I will build.

The directive was framed as non-contingent worth joined to responsibility and physical action.

### Directional Engine

A staged process moving through state awareness, observation, separation, evidence testing, values, choice, action, review, and refinement.

### Emotional Firewall

Core questions include:

- What is this message or interaction asking me to feel before I think?
- What becomes more likely if I accept that feeling as truth?
- What evidence would change my mind?
- Who or what benefits if the activated pattern continues?

### Fracture Mapping

Identify predictable dead zones, triggers, fatigue periods, relational loops, environmental pressures, and failure points. Replace shame with design, support, alternative routes, and repeated testing.

### Hospitaller Protocol

Adapt outer form while preserving useful inner purpose. The historical example remains an analogy and must be presented with qualification.

### Immediate Field

- Body
- Attention
- Environment
- Relationship
- Action

This model became the central organizing architecture of the later book.

## Algorithmic-Age Expansion

The framework examined how digital systems compress the interval between impulse and expression. Speed can create the illusion that organized language equals integrated understanding. Increased technological acceleration therefore requires greater internal discipline, evidence testing, embodied grounding, and relational accountability.

## Religion and Trust

Religion was examined as one form of trust architecture capable of transmitting meaning, ethics, belonging, ritual, identity, and cooperation. The paper rejected simplistic reduction of religion to control and rejected flattening different traditions into a single doctrine.

## Self-Love and Human Development

The framework connected its philosophical Core Directive with self-compassion and self-determination research. Self-love was distinguished from entitlement, avoidance, indiscipline, isolation, and exemption from repair.

## Embodied Sovereignty

Sovereignty was defined as self-direction within biological reality, relationship, responsibility, limitation, and interdependence. It was not defined as total autonomy, emotional invulnerability, or control of every outcome.

## Practical System

### Six-Command Daily Loop

```text
FILTER -> REGULATE -> SEPARATE -> TEST -> CHOOSE -> ITERATE
```

### Ninety-Second Re-entry

Stop new input, slow the exhale, identify physical contact points, name observable facts, identify the emotion and urged behavior, test whether immediate action is necessary, then re-enter.

### Weekly Review

- Which stories guided behavior?
- Where did regulation succeed or fail?
- What measurably improved or worsened?
- What one operating rule should continue, stop, or change?

## Risks and Safeguards

- Do not confuse metaphor with science.
- Do not use nervous-system language to excuse harmful behavior.
- Do not use sovereignty to justify isolation.
- Do not treat every bodily feeling as final truth.
- Do not turn historical recurrence theories into certain predictions.
- Do not replace medical, psychological, legal, or emergency care with the framework.
- Keep the framework able to lose through evidence and consequence.

## Relationship to The Immediate Field

The revised paper became the research and method foundation beneath the current book. The book expands the framework into a reader-centered progression, deeper relational practice, full chapters, lived examples, and the governing promise that agency is reclaimed at the scale of body, attention, environment, relationship, and next action.
